First time back in the ceramics studio after winter break and I decided to throw three large bowls (4 lbs, 6 lbs, and 8 lbs).

The first bowl (4 lbs) was a bit wonky -- it was the first time I had thrown in over a month. However, it worked out and ended up being 8.5" tall by 4.25" wide. I wrapped it up fairly tightly before putting it in the damp room so by Saturday (two days) it needed ~1 hour of air drying before flipping it and wrapping the rim. It dried like that for another ~3 hours.
![bowl1](/img/2019-01-13-throwing-large-bowls/bowl1-small.JPG)

The second bowl (6 lbs) went more smoothly and ended up at 9" x 4.75". It also got ~1 hour of air drying on Saturday. I then flipped it and wrapped the rim and let it dry for another 30 minutes. I then flipped it back over, wet the rim a little bit to prevent cracking (it had dried a touch too much), wrapped it tightly, and popped it into the damp room.
![bowl2](/img/2019-01-13-throwing-large-bowls/bowl2-small.JPG)

When throwing the third bowl (8 lbs, 10" x 6.5") Sarah recommended I center with my forearm rather than hand. This made centering mind-blowingly easier, especially for that amount of clay. This bowl was fairly dry by Saturday, so I was able to flip it and wrap the rim after 30 minutes of air drying. I then had to let the bottom air dry for another ~3.5 hours.
![bowl3](/img/2019-01-13-throwing-large-bowls/bowl3-small.JPG)

**Glaze ideas**
* For at least one of them I plan on doing stripes of glaze using wax masking.
* I might also try a two tone with overlap in the middle.
