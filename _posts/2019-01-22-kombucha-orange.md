My primary kombucha ferment was finally ready to be flavored and bottled yesterday!

It took a couple of weeks, likely due to my room only being about 62 degrees F - kombucha really prefers being closer to 72-75 degrees F, but I haven't bothered to buy a heating mat for it yet.

In the past I've done both blueberry and lemon-ginger for the second ferment. This time around I decided to try orange. Partially because I like orange, but also because I had a bunch of ripe oranges and didn't really feel like going to the store to buy lemon juice.

I first took aside 150 mL of the primary ferment to kickstart the next batch and then I split the rest of the gallon into 7 bottles. This left plenty of space for the flavoring. I hand squeezed the oranges until I got about 140 mL of orange juice. I only hand squeezed them because I don't own a juicer or really any tool of that variety, otherwise I would have done that. Just squeezing by hand didn't get all the juice so I then scooped out the remaining fruit, put it in a cloth (i.e., scrap from an old, but washed, t-shirt) to help squeeze.

I added ~20 mL of orange juice to each bottle and also added a squeeze of honey (~2-3 tsps) to help kickstart the second ferment and add a little extra sweetness.

While doing this I made a new batch of sweet tea for the next brew. I used 6 bags of black tea, 2 bags of green tea, and 1 cup of sugar. I let this steep longer than you would for a cup of tea - maybe 10-15 minutes - to really make sure that I got the nutrients out of the tea as possible. Since it takes awhile for the tea to cool I let it sit overnight before adding the 150 mL of starter from the previous brew. I also add the scoby from the previous brew to my scoby hotel and add ~1 cup of the new sweet tea to the hotel since I wanted the scobys to be completely submerged - plus I figured it would be good to feed them a little.

Data:
- this batch of kombucha had a pH of 3.16 at 18.6 degrees C.
- used 4-5 oranges (forgot to count) to get 140 mL of juice.
- got 7 bottles.

Ideas:
- if the orange flavor works, experiment with adding vanilla beans!
