I finally made a second set of mitered dovetails that I am almost okay with. There are still some nontrivial gaps that I'd like to get rid of in the final project. However, I'm much happier with the overall look of this layout.

<figure>
  <img src="/img/2019-01-29-cherry-mitered-dovetail/rough-glue.JPG" alt="rough glue up" height="50%" width="50%"/>
</figure>

<figure>
  <img src="/img/2019-01-29-cherry-mitered-dovetail/dovetail.JPG" alt="dovetails" height="50%" width="50%"/>
</figure>

I used a 1/3 angle and first marked out the larger 4 tails (two on each end). I liked the idea of having sets of dovetails with a larger gap (pin) in between and for this layout I just eyeballed it. I then divided up the space between the larger tails such that the resulting middle tail wouldn't get too thin.

<figure>
  <img src="/img/2019-01-29-cherry-mitered-dovetail/miter.JPG" alt="miter" height="50%" width="50%"/>
</figure>

I think for the final piece I'd like to have a slightly larger gap between sets of dovetails (maybe 3 sets across the full span). I may also want the pins to be a little thinner. Unfortunately, I'll need to wait to see what the final width of the sideboard will be until I can really start planning different layouts.

Note: The gaps here might be able to be filled with some dust and glue so I've been saving walnut dust as I begin to process the lumber.
